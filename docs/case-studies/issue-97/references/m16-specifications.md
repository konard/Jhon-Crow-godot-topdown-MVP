# M16 Assault Rifle Technical Specifications

## Overview

The M16 rifle is a lightweight, 5.56 mm, air-cooled, gas-operated, magazine-fed assault rifle with a rotating bolt. Developed by Eugene Stoner at ArmaLite in the late 1950s, it has become one of the most widely used rifles globally.

## History

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1957 | AR-15 design begins at ArmaLite |
| 1959 | Design licensed to Colt |
| 1962 | Adopted by U.S. Air Force as M16 |
| 1964 | XM16E1 enters U.S. military service |
| 1965 | Deployed to Vietnam |
| 1967 | M16A1 introduced |
| 1969 | M16A1 replaces M14 as standard service rifle |
| 1982 | M16A2 introduced |
| Present | Still in service, adopted by 80+ countries |

## Design Features

### Materials
- **Receivers:** 7075 aluminum alloy
- **Barrel, Bolt, Bolt Carrier:** Steel
- **Handguards, Pistol Grip, Buttstock:** Plastics

### Distinctive Visual Features

1. **Carrying Handle** - Top-mounted, contains rear sight
2. **Straight-line Stock** - Recoil spring in stock, reduces muzzle rise
3. **Triangular Handguard** - Original design (A1 and earlier)
4. **Round Handguard** - A2 and later models
5. **Flash Suppressor** - Bird-cage style (A1+)
6. **Forward Assist** - Added in A1 model
7. **Magazine Release** - Button on right side
8. **Pistol Grip** - Angled, plastic

### Recoil Design
The M16 uses a "straight-line" recoil design where:
- Recoil spring located in stock behind action
- Serves dual function: operating spring and recoil buffer
- Stock in line with bore reduces muzzle rise
- Enables faster follow-up shots
- Reduces user fatigue

## Variants

### M16 (Original, 1962)
- Triangular handguard
- Three-pronged flash suppressor
- Full-automatic fire only
- No chrome lining
- No forward assist

### M16A1 (1967)
- Bird-cage flash suppressor
- Chrome-lined bore and chamber
- Forward assist added
- Improved reliability
- Vietnam-era standard

### M16A2 (1982)
- Heavier barrel
- Revised rifling (1:7 twist)
- Three-round burst replaces full-auto
- Improved rear sight
- Round handguard
- Chambered for SS109/M855 ammunition

### M16A3
- M16A2 with M16A1 trigger group
- Full-automatic capability restored
- Limited production (Navy SEALs)

### M16A4
- Removable carrying handle
- Picatinny rail for optics
- Improved accessory mounting
- Current U.S. military standard

## Combat Performance

### Advantages
- Lightweight compared to predecessors
- Low recoil
- High accuracy
- Modular design
- Easy to maintain (when properly cleaned)
- High magazine capacity

### Historical Issues (Early Models)
- Jamming in humid conditions
- Carbon buildup with original powder
- Required chrome lining (added in A1)
- Needed proper cleaning kits

## Legacy

- Longest continuously serving rifle in U.S. military history
- Adopted by 80+ countries
- Production exceeds 8 million units
- 5.56×45mm NATO became standard cartridge worldwide
- Basis for countless variants and derivatives
